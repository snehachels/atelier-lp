# ATELIER: BRAND GUIDELINES & POSITIONING
## Your Dancer-First Community Platform

---

## ATELIER: What It Means

**Atelier** (French: ah-tell-YAY)
- A workshop or studio where artists/craftspeople create
- Associated with high fashion ateliers, ballet companies, art studios
- Implies: creativity, collaboration, artistry, craft
- Conveys: "A place where dancers make art together"

**Why this word for dancers:**
- Ballet tradition (Atelier de Danse in classical ballet)
- Fashion/art world connection (dancers understand fashion, design, aesthetics)
- Implies serious craft (not casual, not transactional)
- International, cultured, sophisticated
- Works for all dance styles (classical to street, hip-hop to contemporary)

## ATELIER: BRAND IDENTITY

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**
- **Charcoal/Dark Gray:** #2C2C2C (sophisticated, professional)
- **Rose Gold/Dusty Pink:** #D4A5A5 (creative, elegant, dance-culture)

**Secondary Colors:**
- **Cream/Off-White:** #F5F3F0 (warm, creative workspace feel)
- **Deep Burgundy:** #6B3C3C (accent, sophisticated)
- **Warm Gray:** #9B9B9B (supporting)

**Why these colors:**
- NOT bright tech colors (Google, Instagram blue)
- Feels like a creative studio, not a tech startup
- Sophisticated and intentional
- Warm + professional (not cold)
- Works for both minimalist and artistic brands

### Typography

**Primary Font:** 
- **Montserrat** (clean, modern, geometric sans-serif)
- Used for: Headlines, UI, brand
- Why: Sophisticated but accessible, modern but timeless

**Secondary Font:**
- **Crimson Text** (serif, editorial, classical)
- Used for: Body copy, storytelling, values
- Why: Evokes classical arts, ballet tradition, printed aesthetic

**Usage:**
- Headlines: Montserrat Bold (60-72px)
- Subheadings: Montserrat SemiBold (28-36px)
- Body: Crimson Text Regular (16-18px)
- UI/Buttons: Montserrat Medium (14-16px)

### Logo Concept

**Option 1: Minimal Mark**
- Simple A (for Atelier) 
- Composed of movement/dance shapes
- Can stand alone or with wordmark

**Option 2: Dance-Inspired**
- Circle of dancers (collaborative)
- Enclosed in a square (studio/space)
- Wordmark below: "Atelier"

**Option 3: Type-Based**
- Bold "Atelier" wordmark
- Custom A with subtle dance motif
- Clean, editorial, sophisticated

**Recommendation:** Option 1 or 2 (more distinctive than pure wordmark)

**Logo Colors:**
- Primary version: Dark gray on white
- Reverse: Cream on dark gray
- Accent version: Rose gold on dark gray (for highlights)

## ATELIER: VOICE & TONE

### How Atelier Speaks

**Atelier's Voice:**
- Respectful of the craft (dance is serious art)
- Encouraging but authentic (no toxic positivity)
- Insider language (dancers understand dance culture)
- Professional but warm (not corporate)
- Story-driven (share real dancer stories)

### Example Copy Styles

**DON'T:**
- "Easy booking in 3 clicks!" (too transactional)
- "Join the movement!" (clichéd startup language)
- "Disrupt the dance industry" (not your vibe)
- "Find your tribe" (too casual)

**DO:**
- "Where serious dancers create together"
- "Your studio, your community, your art"
- "Dancers know. This is what we've been asking for."
- "A home for the work"
- "Where rehearsals become collaborations"

### Writing Examples

**Example 1: About Us**
```
Atelier is built on a simple belief: dancers deserve a home where they can create, collaborate, and perform.

Not a marketplace. Not a transaction. A real community—where your art comes first.

We started with 50+ conversations with professional dancers. They told us what they needed. This is what we built.
```

**Example 2: Feature Description**
```
Find Collaborators
Stop scrolling Instagram. Find the dancers you need—the right style, the right energy, the right vision.
```

**Example 3: Class Listing**
```
Choreography Lab with Maya
Master class in contemporary movement and composition. Limited to 8 dancers. Saturdays at Riverside Studio.
```

## ATELIER: VISUAL STYLE

### Photography Style
- **Real dancers**, not stock photos
- **Unpolished but intentional** (rehearsal candids, not photoshoots)
- **Diverse bodies, styles, backgrounds** (all dancers welcome)
- **In motion or in creative thought** (not posing)
- **Studio lighting** (warm, studio feel—not sunny/outdoorsy)

### Imagery Examples
- Dancers mid-rehearsal, focused, in motion
- Small group collaborating, looking at choreography notes
- Teacher adjusting a student's form
- Dancers sitting in circle, planning
- Close-up of hands on a studio floor
- Empty studio before class (inviting, potential)

### Design Elements
- **Whitespace:** Generous, breathing room (creative workspace feel)
- **Grid:** Clean, organized, intentional layouts
- **Accents:** Rose gold lines, borders, highlights (not heavy-handed)
- **Typography emphasis:** Use scale and weight to direct attention
- **No gradients or effects:** Flat, intentional design

## ATELIER: LAUNCH POSITIONING SUMMARY

### What You'll Say
"**Atelier** is a creative community platform for dancers. We help dancers collaborate, take classes, book rehearsal spaces, and perform—all in one place. Built by dancers, for dancers. Not a marketplace. A home."

### What People Will Understand
- Sophisticated, artist-focused
- Serious about dance (not casual)
- Community-centered
- Exclusive-feeling but inclusive
- Professional + creative

### Who You're Targeting (First)
- Professional dancers (choreographers, teachers, performers)
- Serious amateurs (dancers who practice 4+ days/week)
- Teachers wanting to reach students
- Performers wanting to find collaborators
- NOT: casual dancers, beginners (though they're welcome later)

### Why Atelier (vs. DanceHub)
- **More sophisticated** (attracts serious artists)
- **More differentiated** (Atelier > generic Hub)
- **Deeper cultural resonance** (ballet tradition)
- **Attracts quality founders** (dance-serious people)
- **More defensible brand** (unique, not commoditized)
